How to Target Voters for a Political Campaign

Summary

Every political campaign strategy is built around targeting voters through different stages of voter contact, and while there is a range of ways to do the voter contact they all follow the similar voter targeting strategy.

Stages of a Political Campaign Targeting

  1. ID : This can be as simple as using party registration or as complex as running a grassroots effort where you survey voter at the door and on the phone. This will allow you to create voter universes, so you can focus more on undecided voters, and supporters

  2. Persuade : This part is often skipped, but it allows the political campaign to better refine its voter universes and allows political campaign to reduce the overall size of the undecided voter universe

  3. GOTV : This is the last part of any political campaign, and it was the campaign tries to contact their support universes as many times as they can before the polls close.

Getting Started with Voter Targeting

The First thing you will need to start voter targeting is a voter registration list for this article I will be using a DC voter registration list from 2014.

Overview of Voter List

Before we talk about targeting voter with a voter registration list we should go over the basic of every voter registration list that includes.

Personal Information

The first thing you will notice is that you are given personal information, and in some state, it can include phone numbers and email address. Thing to keep in mind the information might not be current for voters (ie. moved or new number), and can have more people at each address then there are because of this. While it is a safe bet that a voter with an A, which means active, in status information is current inactive voter noted with an I will have to be followed up on and fixed.

The way you do this is by contacting and updating political campaign list through voter contact.

Voting Information

This part of the list can change based on the state you are in, but there are some common elements about each list.

The most important part of this section is how often they have voted. This is because research has shown that people who vote are more likely to vote again. The best voter is the 3 of 4 and 4 of 4 because they will likely vote without much voter contact need.

The other thing that will be listed is their party registration (shown with personal information). In a state that doesnâ€™t have party registration you are given information on which primaries, they have voted in(ie. democrat or republican). Then you can make a 5 point partisan scale, or you just mark them down as republican, democrat, and crossover.

Data Brokers

The final thing you can do to target voter is to buy voter data from a vendor, but the major thing to keep in mind is that the voter data is most likely 3rd hand data (.ie magazine subscriptions and email lists) while these can give you some information about a voter you donâ€™t know how old the data is and could include people who donâ€™t live there

Running surveys through canvassing and phone banking is the only way you can make sure the information is correct, which makes 2nd hand data (.ie Facebook and LinkedIn) give you better results.
